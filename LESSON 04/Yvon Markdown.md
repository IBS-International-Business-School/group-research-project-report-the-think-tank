Review of Job Satisfaction
Job satisfaction describes how happy and fulfilled employees feel about their work. It’s shaped by many factors—such as the work environment, management style, flexibility, relationships, and work-life balance. In recent years, new work models like remote and hybrid setups have changed how people experience satisfaction at work.
Office Work


In traditional offices, satisfaction often comes from social interaction, teamwork, and direct feedback. Many employees value the structure and sense of community an office provides. However, long commutes, fixed schedules, and workplace stress can lower satisfaction for some.


Remote Work
Remote employees often report higher satisfaction due to flexibility, autonomy, and better balance between work and personal life. Yet, challenges like isolation, communication gaps, and blurred work boundaries can reduce overall happiness—especially when support and clear expectations are lacking.


Hybrid Work
Hybrid work, which combines both remote and office days, offers a middle ground. It gives employees flexibility while maintaining social contact. When managed well, it can lead to strong satisfaction. Still, unclear policies or unequal treatment between remote and office workers can cause frustration.
